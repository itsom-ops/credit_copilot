# Bank Lending Policy Guidelines

## [Section 1.0] Credit Score Requirements
*   **[Section 1.1] Prime Status**: Applicants with a credit score of 720 or above are generally approved for prime lending rates.
*   **[Section 1.2] Sub-prime Status**: Applicants with a credit score between 620 and 719 require secondary manual review.
*   **[Section 1.3] Rejection Criteria**: Any applicant with a credit score below 620 must be automatically rejected.

## [Section 2.0] Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio
The Debt-to-Income ratio is calculated as: `(Total Monthly Debt / Total Monthly Income) * 100`
*   **[Section 2.1] Optimal DTI**: DTI <= 36%. Maximum approval probability.
*   **[Section 2.2] Acceptable DTI**: DTI > 36% and <= 43%. May require additional collateral or higher interest rates.
*   **[Section 2.3] High Risk DTI**: DTI > 43%. Auto-reject unless the applicant has a credit score > 750 and liquid assets exceeding the loan amount.

## [Section 3.0] Employment Verification
*   **[Section 3.1] Salaried**: Salaried employees must have a minimum employment history of 12 months.
*   **[Section 3.2] Self-Employed**: Self-employed individuals must provide 2 years of consistent tax returns and have a credit score > 680 to be considered.

## [Section 4.0] Loan-to-Value (LTV) Limits
The Loan-to-Value ratio measures the loan amount against the property value.
*   **[Section 4.1] Standard Mortgages**: Maximum LTV is 80% without Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI).
*   **[Section 4.2] High LTV Exceptions**: LTV up to 95% is permitted for first-time homebuyers with a credit score > 740 and DTI < 35%.

## [Section 5.0] Co-signers and Guarantors
*   **[Section 5.1] Eligibility**: Co-signers must have an individual credit score of 700 or greater and reside in the same country.
*   **[Section 5.2] Impact on Risk**: A qualified co-signer offsets a primary applicant's high DTI, but it does NOT override the absolute minimum credit score requirement of 620 for the primary applicant.

## [Section 6.0] Bankruptcy and Foreclosures
*   **[Section 6.1] Chapter 7**: Minimum 4-year waiting period from the discharge date before a new mortgage application can be approved.
*   **[Section 6.2] Foreclosure**: Minimum 7-year waiting period from the completion date of a foreclosure.
